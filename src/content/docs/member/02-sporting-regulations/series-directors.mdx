## Definition

**Series Directors** are defined as the person/people responsible for enforcing league rules during races, and in the league Discord Server. Series Directors responsibilities include, but are not limited to: **penalty management, caution management, race flow management, rule enforcement, general conduct monitoring, and stat/scoring management**.

## Scope

1. **Series Directors** reserve the right to give any driver deemed to be a threat to competition a disqualification.
2. **Series Directors** reserve the right to give any driver deemed to be the cause of one or more incidents the appropriate number of RC points.
3. **Series Directors** reserve the right to disqualify a driver if they are deemed at fault of 3 separate incidents caused during the course of a single race.
4. **Series Directors** reserve the right to throw a yellow flag if, in the opinion of the **Series Directors**, the incident impacted the field severely.
5. **Automatic cautions** will be used at all oval courses. **Manual cautions** will be used at all road courses. **Series Directors** are required to throw cautions for incidents with **three or more cars** on oval tracks in which iRacing did not initiate a caution, or incidents with **five or more cars** on road courses.
6. For series with stage cautions, **Series Directors** will throw the caution at the end of the predetermined lap once the 10th place car crosses the start/finish line. At road courses, the caution will be thrown when the leader enters the final turn.
7. Pit lane will close when the leader reaches **2 laps remaining in the stage**.
8. If a caution occurs **3 laps or less** remaining in the stage at a superspeedway or **5 laps or less** at any other track, that caution will end the stage and points will be awarded.
